## Learning Outcomes

Upon completing Precalculus, students will be able to:

- Work with arithmetic and geometric series using sigma notation
- Analyze rational functions including asymptotes, holes, and end behavior
- Solve quadratic, polynomial, and non-polynomial inequalities
- Apply inverse trigonometric functions, identities, and sum/difference/double-angle formulas
- Solve trigonometric equations including transformed and advanced cases
- Perform vector operations in 2D and 3D including dot product and cross product
- Work with matrices including multiplication, determinants, inverses, and linear transformations
- Analyze conic sections: ellipses and hyperbolas
- Work with parametric and polar equations
- Operate with complex numbers in rectangular and polar form, including De Moivre's Theorem
- Apply probability concepts including discrete random variables and the normal distribution
- Perform statistical analysis including correlation, regression, and data interpretation
