# AP Calculus BC

> Master the fundamentals of single-variable calculus including with vectors, parametric and polar equations. Learn how to apply tests of convergence to infinite series and to approximate functions using Taylor series.

**Prerequisites:** Precalculus or Integrated Math III (Honors)

## Learning Outcomes

Upon completing AP Calculus BC, students will be able to:

- Master all AP Calculus AB content (limits, derivatives, integrals, differential equations)
- Apply advanced integration techniques: integration by parts, partial fractions, improper integrals
- Solve logistic growth differential equations and use Euler's method
- Differentiate and integrate parametric equations and vector-valued functions
- Differentiate polar curves and calculate areas bounded by polar curves
- Analyze planar motion of particles using calculus
- Determine convergence of infinite series using multiple tests
- Construct and analyze Taylor and Maclaurin series
- Apply the Lagrange error bound for Taylor polynomial approximations
- Calculate arc lengths of planar, parametric, and polar curves
