# Mathematical Foundations I

> Mathematical Foundations I is the first course in a sequence specially crafted for adults seeking the most direct path to prepare for university math courses. The course starts with fraction arithmetic and covers all the way up through the algebra of linear equations. Upon completing the course, students will be prepared for Mathematical Foundations II, the second step of the journey towards university-level math.

## Learning Outcomes

Upon successful completion of this course, students will have mastered the following:

### Arithmetic Operations
- Use number lines to visualize addition, subtraction, and negative numbers.
- Leverage equivalent fractions as a strategy to add and subtract fractions.
- Generalize previous understandings of multiplication and division to multiply and divide fractions.
- Interpret exponents as repeated multiplication and radicals as the opposite of exponents.
- Understand the relationship between fractional exponents and radicals.
- Apply the order of operations to evaluate arithmetic expressions.

### Measurement and Data
- Convert between units of measurement and compute unit rates.
- Understand the relationship between whole, part, and percentage.

### Prime Factorization
- Express a composite integer as the product of its prime factors.
- Leverage prime factorization as a strategy to compute greatest common factors and least common multiples.

### Linear Equations and Inequalities
- Understand the concept of a variable as a placeholder in a mathematical expression or verbal phrase.
- Create equations that describe relationships between quantities.
- Understand rules of algebra as a process of reasoning and use them to solve linear equations, inequalities, and systems.
- Relate the solutions of two-variable equations and inequalities to graphs in the coordinate plane.
- Set up and solve systems of linear equations in real-world modeling contexts.

### Nonlinear Equations and Inequalities
- Leverage multiplication as a strategy to transform rational equations into linear equations.
- Extend knowledge of radicals and solving linear equations to handle cases when the variable is raised to a power.
- Relate absolute value to distance on the number line and apply this understanding to solve absolute value equations and inequalities.

### Polynomials
- Leverage the distributive property and an understanding of like terms to manipulate polynomial expressions.
- Understand the relationship between multiplication and factoring and apply a systematic method to factor trinomials with leading coefficients.

### Functions
- Identify, interpret, and build functions that model relationships between two quantities.
- Represent and reason about functions using formal notation.
- Visualize functions in the coordinate plane by graphing.

### Geometry
- Classify triangles and quadrilaterals based on their properties.
- Use geometric relationships to solve for unknown angle measures and side lengths.
- Compute area and perimeter of circles, triangles, and various types of quadrilaterals.
- Calculate side lengths and areas of special right triangles using geometric reasoning and the Pythagorean theorem.
